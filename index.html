I am Pranay Mathur, a graduate robotics student at Georgia Tech. My research interests lie at the intersection of computer-vision, perception and deep-learning with application in robotics. The overarching goal of my work is to achieve robust full-stack robot autonomy. I completed my undergraduate degree at BITS, Pilani where I majored in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering.

I worked as an open-source developer at CROSS as part of the Google Summer of Code '22 program, on the project Path-Finding using OpenCV and ML. Prior to this, I worked with Addverb Technologies as a perception engineer on vision-based navigation and localization techniques. I have also interned at Technoyantra where I developed a localization algorithm using EKF based fusion of pose estimates from fiducial tags and LIDAR based SLAM. In the preceeding summer, I interned at KPIT where I worked on deep-learning based multi-modal sensor fusion for object detection in adverse weather conditions. I completed my undergraduate thesis at the Autonomous Robots Lab under the guidance of Prof. Kostas Alexis, where I developed a generalizable Resource-Aware algorithm for deployment of Visual Inertial Odometry algorithms on aerial vehicles with computationally constrained onboard systems.

I am an avid aviation enthusiast and love building and flying quadcopters. Apart from aviation, I am a voracious reader and love the work of Rudyard Kipling, Roald Dahl and Jeffrey Archer. I love playing football and running marathons so feel free to reach out if you need a running partner!

Feel free to check out my CV and drop me an e-mail if you want to chat with me!
